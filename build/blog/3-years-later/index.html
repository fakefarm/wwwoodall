3 years later

Jan 13, 2015

I attended a three month code school in Spring 2012. I can't speak for everyone but the reason I attended is because I saw the value in code as an end result with my t-shirt company. Then, I tried teaching myself by watching lynda, attending meetups and other things. The problem was I wasn't surrounded with other developers, had no context, and was just not going fast enough to learn.

Code Academy gave me a new context, ramped me up, and taught me some fundamentals that are hard to learn solo, or at a workplace.

Part of the self-taught mentality is trying to figure out what to learn and how much of it. It's a broad search to find the right information. No question this will be a lengthy journey to learn what you need to know in this new country called Internetland.

Now that hacker academies have been around for a few years I have had the opportunity to talk with some new graduates who are looking for employment. I contrast what how they talk and my own journey and have come up with a few bits of wisdom.
