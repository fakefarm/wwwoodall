wrong righteousness

Dec 20, 2014

I've been drinking more than I feel comfortable. I conclude it's a combination of free beer, cultural routines, and a way to check out from stress. I've also been thinking about how I should help people. I want to help people but I don't really know the right way. I over-think it and end up not doing much.

This morning I was talking to the Lord about it and concluded that if He would just let me start helping and stop drinking then I would be pretty happy with myself.

He asked why do I want to start and stop these things? I felt I had an obvious answer in that they would make me more Christ-like. In short, I would become more righteous.

I took my conclusion a step further and began to imagine success. What would I say when I achieve helping people and being sober? Well, I could credit my righteousness to this list of things I do and don't do. "And you too can find righteousness if you buy my book for $19.95!"

My desire for good and bad habit management reveals the deeper issue at play;

my tendency is to wrongly
equate righteousness to performance.

That's the wrong direction. What I do and who I am are two different topics. How my good habits grow, and bad habits die, is not related to righteousness. What I need to re-settle is how to properly define my worth.

At that point the Spirit graciously reminds me to recognize that I am righteous, even while drinking and not helping people, because of the righteousness He gave me.
