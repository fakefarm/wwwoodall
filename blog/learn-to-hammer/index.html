Learn to hammer

Jan 07, 2015

The difference between education and training is the end result. Education is for knowledge that may or not be useful. Training is a ridged process with a specific goal in mind. THe outcome is a development of new skills. Skills are practical activities that are practiced and there is noticeable difference between a skilled and unskilled individual of the same task. Educations attempt at this is grades but there are few real benefits of this effort.

Take learning to hammer. There are two ways you can do it. You can learn the history of the hammer. View pictures of the first hammer. There could even be a quiz about the various weights and purposes of each hammer. But you will not achieve the purpose of a hammer until you start using it. You need to swing and miss. You need to bend a few nails. And you may even need to learn what it feels like to hit your thumb. After you get through these bad stages, you'll begin to get better through practice.

The same goes for learning to code. The best way is to just start writing a lot of bad code. Take for example learning HTML. There are roughly 105 different words (elements) in the language. You don't need to know them all to start writing HTML. In fact, you could write the wrong words until you learn otherwise. This is the idea of learning English as your first vs. second language. We know that 2nd language people learn and have a very ridged sound that may be correct, but it's not how we speak. You will refine the language as you write it. Your vocabulary and structure will improve the more you practice.

In first learning to code, don't stress about it being accurate. Just make it work. Getting the nail into a board without denting the wall or your head is the goal. Writing code that works is your first goal.
