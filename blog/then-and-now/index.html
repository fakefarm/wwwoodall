then and now

Jan 27, 2015

I started learning to code in order to build my own business. My first few projects were HTML and CSS with a few rails tutorials. When I decided to go to Code Academy I assumed I would ramp up and take my new skill back to running a business and coding on the side.

What I quickly discovered is that coding is a full time operation. There was no way I was going to do both. But by this time I had caught the coding bug. So when it came time to chose between business or coding. I chose coding.

So for the past three years I've been motivated by learning what I need to learn and then launch out on a new business. Thinking that once I could see the complete picture, I figured I'd be ready to dive in.

I've been at LivingSocial for a year now. What I've realized is that no single person can wrap their arms around the entire system. At first, this was depressing. I questioned if my motivation would falter at realizing that what I'm pursuing is the equivalent of reaching the moon by car.

After thinking this through, I've begun to see this as a good thing. First and most tangible is that there is always more work to be done at the day job. So, I'm not at risk of being unemployed anytime soon. yay!

Second, is those who want to go into business need to work well with people. If you cannot find a business partner or vendor network you enjoy working with, then you shouldn't go into business.

Third, if it were up to me, I'd rather build a product than a company. I'd prefer to be the technical co-founder and let my partner grow the customer base and other vital aspects.
