Sites for Startups

Jan 07, 2015

I managed three different t-shirt companies with Basecamp. My love for Basecamp introduced me to Ruby on Rails. Ruby on Rails introduced me to the pattern known as 'CRUD'. CRUD stands for Create, Read, Update, and Delete. CRUD is the pattern for nearly every feature you build as a web app. While there are unique aspects of every project, this is what the customer will experience doing.

For example with Twitter.

  • Create a new tweet
  • Read Tweets
  • Update your (draft) tweet
  • Delete drunk tweets

After seeing this pattern, I began to wonder how many different patterns are using CRUD. Are we using extra mental ram by re-inventing the wheel? It got me thinking how I would manage a consulting business. I would probably convert technical terms into products that consumers (and my future customers) use and interact with. So, I made this diagram.

If this was targeted toward a startup, then I'd try to pinpoint the generic core of their initial service. Is it for public or private use? Is the purpose to provide content or communication? To me, I see that businesses start from one of 7 spots. (That is step 1.)

Step two is layering on the different types of common CRUDs. What are we Creating? Blogs? Orders? Tasks? Messages? All of these things are CRUD.

The other steps included adding style business specific content to differentiate one self.
