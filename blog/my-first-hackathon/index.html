My first hackathon

Feb 15, 2015

My first hackathon at starter league was an idea to create a page based off hashtags you use most often on twitter. We called it a hashpage. There were 5 of us on the founding team and we had a weekend to build it.

Interview due diligence

LivingSocial is a big company so we are regularly interviewing candidates. once you pass a technical portion you then come in for a face to face interview for cultural fit. So, those who conduct those interviews take it on themselves to do some due diligence. That's when Twitter becomes an informal way to see what kind of person you are. What are you into? How do you present yourself? Will we like working with you? It's wild to think that people can and do get judged by their twitter feed but it's true.

It got me thinking how it would be cool to have a one page summary I could type in a handle and see a snapshot what the individual is into. That's a little different than hashpage's purpose but it made me wonderwhat it would take for me to build something like that three years later. So, I took about two hours to try it. I was pleasantly surprised to discover that I accomplished more in my mini-hack than our 5 team did over a 72 hour period!

So far, I have a basic proof of concept. All you do is type in a twitter handle and it will reply with number of tweets. The twitter access part is now done. I'm going to be turning my attention to processing and presenting those tweets in a cool way.
