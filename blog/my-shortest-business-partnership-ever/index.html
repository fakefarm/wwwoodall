My shortest business partnership ever

Feb 12, 2015

An unedited (except typos and punctuation) text transcript between me and an artist who I've wondered if we'd go into business together.

Me:

Got a logo project I want to work with you on. Want to do it together? For a paying client.

Future Business Partner:

Let's talk! I have bandwidth.

Me:

Let's act like we have a business and practice how we'd work together.

Future Business Partner:

Of course we would act professional.

Or should we act like savages and try to scam the other person!

Me:

I’m tracking time from her first contact to see how long it takes to complete (or lose) the business.

Let’s say I want to keep her as my client but I want to contract you out for some art services, like a logo. Do you want to do that and if so, how?

I’m going to invoice her as hireDave b/c she wants me to do a few other website related tasks

here’s her first question to me;

Client:

What's the going rate for this kind of graphical work btw? and can you guys do other things like social media graphics formatted for Twitter posts, Pinterest, FB etc to promote new blog posts, etc.

Me:

Going back to our walk talk, I want to tell her an hourly rate and get to work. I don’t want to tell her ‘it depends.’

I’m thinking $75 hr. for you, with a billable rate of $125 hr. to her. What say you?

Business Partner:

It depends. Ha! I say we should talk for at least 15-20 before we solidify this. It can't be hashed out in a text.

Me:

Who do I bill that 15 - 20m to?

Business Partner:

Yourself. My fist hour of consultation is free.

Me:

Mine isn’t.

Business Partner:

Do you want to meet or not?

Me:

Honestly, no I don’t want to meet. I want to get to work.

I can’t afford to give out 10 hours of free consultation to 9 leads who say ‘no’ and the one that says ‘yes’

So I’m starting with her with the details (that she asked for) and she’ll most likely say 'no'.

Business Partner:

I think you are joking but I can't do this over a text.

Me:

For business, I want to ‘spend’ as little as possible and ‘make’ as much as possible.

But as friends.. Yes, I want to hang out and meet :-)

Business is details - who, what, where, when, why, how. and all of those things I want to spend as little time as possible doing and managing

So, I push that as much as possible to forms, links, attachments, contracts, and such

Seriously, I would like to have responded to her by now with - “It’s $125 an hour and takes about 10 - 20 hours to make a logo. the next step is to setup a proposal of work if you’re interested. we’d love to work with you!”

She says ‘yes or no’ and we move on or start working on billable time.

Business Partner:

Call me when you are free or let me know a time we can match up to talk.

Me:

You’re making it hard to hire you.

Business Partner:

You are making it hard to want to work with you. I'm trying to stay professional. The quickness you want now can be there as we learn to work together but it will take a little effort at the beginning. These passive aggressive lessons in business over texts are not appealing.

Me:

Totally makes sense. I think this is what I wanted to explore if we gelled or not. It doesn’t seem like we are and that’s okay.

Business Partner:

I'm $100 hr. And I can do most logos in 20 hours. If it goes over 20 hours. I'll bill you until it's done. If it's under I'll bill you for what I did.

I think you wanted a well oiled machine that haven't even been assembled yet

Me:

No, it is well oiled and it is assembled and I knew it. You know how to concept, design, and how long things takes. You just didn’t cut to the chase.

Business Partner:

I'm about to sit down. I can be to your office in 5 if you want to talk. If not we will have to say I'm out for this one if you need to give your answer now.

Me:

I can’t talk b/c I’m at work, which is the other reason I’m cutting the chase. I have to do this on the side.

OK. Let’s call this a good practice run and stop now. I have nothing but love for you and I have no hard feelings. Sorry if I’m overly pushy!

Former Business Partner:

Love back at you.
