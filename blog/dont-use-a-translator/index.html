Don't use a translator

Dec 09, 2014

Are you a coder? Then you should make a blog from scratch. Don't use a translator like WordPress, Tumblr, or ghost. You know how to read and write code and should let your voice be heard, unhindered.

You might think that those platforms have done a lot of work on your behalf, but they haven't. A blog platform is built for people who don't know how to code, to do the things we coders can do in our sleep. Posts, tags, pretty urls, and such. These concepts are second nature to you. But if they aren't, well, you should take it as a challenge for them to become so.

When you build your own blog you are in control of what you say and how you say it. Your site stands out because it's not a template and demonstrates to other developers that you know your craft. You will learn all kinds of things in a safe environment and don't have to wonder if the content you write is yours or the social media platform you chose.

Probably the core driver for me learning to code was to stop being bound by a translator. The translator metaphor rings true as I begin to kick-start my blogging for the 4th time. I started on a quest to build my own blog with jekyll and then rails. While they let me be in control, they still had limitations. I have ultimately settled on middleman with this site/blog and I recommend you try middleman for your custom-blog project.

Feel free to hit me up if you have questions with your own future site.
