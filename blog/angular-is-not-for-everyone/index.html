Angular is not for everyone.

Jan 15, 2015

This is a rambling journal entry

About 10 years ago people didn't have dev tools but they still had to develop on the web. Those people like Jeffrey Zeldman and others have defined the web standards because they dove into the season of code they were. Our version of that is known as 'the javascript framework wars.'

There's a part of your career you are living through called the javascript framework wars. This is because nobody has won the way Rails won 8 - 10 years ago. So, we're searching and looking for solutions. I like the coding horror law that everything that could be written JS eventually will be.

If you're a real front end web developer then JavaScript is a requirement of the job. So people are complaining about the turbulence and the terror of spending time being confused. Well guess what, people are also paying you to learn these languages. and If you are like the average programmer then you enjoy learning new things. Keep your eyes on the ball - You need to learn javascript like the back of your hand. That means dabbling with Angular, node, bower, grunt, gulp, angular, ember, react and more.

We're at a crossroads where front and back-end developers are needing to make a choice. We all started at Rails and loved it but then we had to boost something about rails - the front side or the back side. The back end guys chose to look into Scala, Clojure, go, or other. The front end guys are moving from JQuery and looking at frameworks. It's as if Rails is the centralizer that fosters the community, but then what faction to you attend next? Elixer with Jose?

What I think will come of this is the the genuine differentiation between a front and back-end developer. The front-end developer knows HTMl, JS, CSS, JSON, Dev Tools, Some rails. Rails may stay or it may be the casualty of front end option for static sites and abandon the views, and the back-end guys abandoning active record in favor of Go or clojure.
